EVENT RECAP / LUSAKA

Cursor Hackathon Zambia Brings AI Builders Together in Lusaka

The recent Cursor Hackathon Zambia organized by Cursor Zambia marked another important step in growing Zambia's local AI builder ecosystem.

Held at The Library Shhh in Lusaka, the event brought together developers, students, creatives, designers, and curious builders for a full day focused on collaboration, rapid prototyping, and experimenting with modern AI development workflows using Cursor.

The hackathon was designed to be beginner friendly while still giving experienced builders space to create ambitious projects. Participants formed teams, explored ideas together, and built projects directly during the event using Cursor AI-assisted development, tools, and workflows.

Building Real Solutions With AI

One of the biggest highlights of the event was the diversity of ideas created throughout the day. A total of 11 projects were successfully built during the hackathon across multiple tracks focused on solving real-world African and Zambian challenges.

Rather than focusing only on theory, the hackathon encouraged participants to ship working concepts, prototypes, and applications that could eventually grow into real startups, tools, and platforms.

Growing Zambia's AI Builder Ecosystem

Beyond the projects themselves, the hackathon represented something bigger: creating spaces where Zambians can actively participate in building the future of AI instead of only consuming technology built elsewhere.

The event emphasized collaboration, accessibility, experimentation, and learning. Beginners were able to work alongside experienced builders, while non-technical participants also contributed through ideas, design thinking, research, and creative direction.

The success of the hackathon also highlighted how AI-powered development tools like Cursor are lowering barriers to entry and allowing more Africans to rapidly prototype and build real-world applications. Through initiatives like Cursor Zambia, Zambia's local AI ecosystem continues to grow, creating new opportunities for builders, innovators, and communities across the country.
