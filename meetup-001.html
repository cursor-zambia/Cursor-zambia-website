EVENT RECAP / LUSAKA

Cursor Activation Meetup, Zambia

Join us for the Cursor Activation Meetup in Zambia. A space to discover, build, and connect with developers, founders, students, and curious minds from across Lusaka.

We started with a simple introduction to Cursor, breaking down what it is and how it works. No jargon, no assumptions. Just a clear-eyed look at what AI-native development actually means and why it matters for developers building in Zambia today.

Developers deep in a hands-on session at the meetup.

From there we moved into a live demo, watching real projects being built and debugged right in front of the room. You could feel the energy shift when people saw, for the first time, just how fast ideas can become working code.

"It's also a space to meet developers, founders, students, and curious minds. To share ideas, have real conversations, and start building something together."

After the demo, attendees got a chance to try it themselves in a hands-on session, exploring what Cursor can do and how AI-native development actually feels when you're in the seat. Come with your laptops and curiosity, ready to connect.

The room was a mix of seasoned engineers, career switchers, students, and founders, all drawn by the same question: what does building with AI actually look like in practice? By the end of the session, most of them had an answer.

An attendee working through the hands-on session.

What made the evening special wasn't just the technology. It was the people. Conversations kept going long after the formal session ended. New collaborations started. Ideas were exchanged. The Cursor Zambia community had its first real gathering, and it was just the beginning.

The Cursor community gathering in Lusaka.

The energy was undeniable. From first-timers picking up Cursor for the very first time, to experienced developers rethinking their entire workflow, everyone left with something new. That's what a community is for.
